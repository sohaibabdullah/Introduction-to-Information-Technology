# CSE 5000 – Information to Information Technology  
**Program:** MBA  
**Duration:** 12 Weeks  
**Contact Hours:** 2 × 1 hour 20 minutes per week  

---

## Course Description
This course introduces MBA students to the **technical foundations of modern information technology systems** that underpin contemporary organizations. Moving beyond high-level business discussions, the course focuses on **how information is represented, processed, stored, transmitted, and secured** within digital systems.

The course is **highly practical and demonstration-oriented**, combining live system walkthroughs, hands-on labs, and applied case analysis with strong academic rigor. Students will develop the technical literacy required to communicate with IT professionals, evaluate technology decisions, and lead digital initiatives.

---

## Course Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Explain how data is transformed into information and knowledge within IT systems  
2. Understand core computing infrastructure: hardware, operating systems, networks, and cloud platforms  
3. Work hands-on with databases, SQL, analytics tools, and cloud services at a managerial-technical level  
4. Analyze enterprise systems (ERP, CRM) from a data-flow and integration perspective  
5. Understand software development processes and IT project lifecycles  
6. Identify cybersecurity, privacy, and governance risks  
7. Critically evaluate emerging technologies such as AI, automation, and digital platforms  

---

## Intended Audience
- MBA and Executive MBA students  
- Students without a formal IT or computer science background  
- Future managers, consultants, entrepreneurs, and policy professionals
